In the process, I'll leave teams ready for continued success by helping them up-skill._

As an experienced software architect and coach, I look at organizations as socio-technical systems where architecture, product design, and human collaboration are deeply intertwined.

## 2. Focused Training

I run target training courses built to equip you with actionable technical and collaboration skills. My current training catalog is structured around five core pillars:

1. **Collaborative Design & Silo-Busting:** Moving software design out of isolated rooms and making it an active, group-wide practice to build shared understanding across product and tech.
2. **Conflict Resolution for Devs (Verbal Aikido):** Communication and interpersonal blockages slow down systems just as much as technical flaws. These practical dojos teach technical profiles how to constructively handle friction, clear relational queues, and preserve psychological safety.
3. **Delivering with Legacy Code through Quantified Technical Product Management:** Stop asking for a full rewrite that will never come. Learn how to map, quantify, and track technical debt so it can be managed as a first-class product metric using iterative, safe refactoring strategies.
4. **Teamwork Around AI & Ensemble Harness Engineering:** How to collaborate productively with generative AI assistants without losing the joy of the craft or giving in to cognitive debt. We explore structured human-AI loops, prompt/context engineering as small batches, and ensemble programming models.
5. **Facilitating Large Groups with Liberating Structures:** Equipping change agents and managers with a simple and reusable facilitation language, to replace meetings with collective intelligence.

## 3. Longer-Term Embedded Consulting

I partner with organizations over extended periods to guide transformations anchored directly in daily software engineering reality. These missions typically focus on:
* **Product & Flow Optimization:** Exposing the invisible queues, eliminating handoff waste, and shifting your product engineering culture from *time to market* to *time to impact*.
* **Domain-Driven Design (DDD):** Re-architecting legacy landscapes into clean, AI-friendly, bounded contexts that decouple dependencies.
* **AI-Augmented Engineering:** Integrating AI tools inside and around engineering teams in an effective and sustainable way.
